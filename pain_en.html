1. Activate the yeast:
In a bowl, mix the dry yeast, sugar, and about 100 ml of warm water.
Let sit for 10 minutes until foamy. (If it doesn’t foam, the yeast is dead or the water was too hot.)

2. Prepare the dough:
In a large mixing bowl, add the gluten-free flour and salt (avoid placing salt directly on the yeast).
Add the yeast mixture, oil, remaining warm water, and cider vinegar (optional).
Mix with a wooden spoon or electric mixer with dough hooks. The dough should be sticky and soft—closer to a thick cake batter than traditional bread dough.

3. Let the dough rise:
Cover the bowl with a clean towel.
Let it rise in a warm place for 1 to 1.5 hours, until it doubles in size.

4. Bake:
Preheat your oven to 200 °C (fan setting).
Pour the dough into a greased or parchment-lined baking pan (loaf or round).
Smooth the surface with a wet spatula, optionally sprinkle with seeds.
Bake for 40–45 minutes. The bread should be golden with a firm crust.
Let cool on a wire rack before slicing.
